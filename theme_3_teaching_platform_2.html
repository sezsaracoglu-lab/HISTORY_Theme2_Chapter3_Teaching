Founding figures
The thread that ties it all together

The chapter argues that the long-standing hierarchy of pure science above applied science is not a methodological fact. It is a social attitude — class-inherited from the European university tradition — that has shaped which research gets funded, which figures get remembered, and which people get included.

The same prejudice that pushed applied psychology to the margins also pushed women to the margins. Both were treated as less serious. The chapter asks you to recognise this pattern, to see how it persists in modern league tables and research assessment, and to evaluate research by intent rather than by inherited prestige.
